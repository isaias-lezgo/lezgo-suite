# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Business owners, sales managers, and marketing directors at SMBs in Mexico and Latin America. They're evaluating whether to switch from spreadsheets or fragmented tools to an all-in-one platform. They visit on a laptop or phone during a work day, have seen plenty of generic SaaS sites before, and are skeptical — they need to feel the product is serious before they book a call or start a trial.

## Product Purpose

Lezgo Suite is an all-in-one CRM, marketing automation, and AI analytics platform for Latin American businesses. The marketing site is where prospects decide whether to trust the brand. Success looks like a qualified lead booking a demo or starting a plan.

## Brand Personality

Ambitious, bold, modern. Speaks directly to the business owner. Confident about outcomes without being arrogant. Spanish-first: built FOR Mexican professionals, not translated for them.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS cream**: Notion-style soft pastels, light gray cards everywhere, safe and forgettable.
- **Enterprise blue**: Salesforce/SAP formality. Too corporate for the SMB owner trying to grow fast.
- **Startup neon**: Dark mode with neon gradients, crypto/Web3 energy. Wrong context entirely.
- **Overcrowded marketing**: Excessive animations, confetti, a CTA on every pixel. Noise that erodes trust.

## Design Principles

1. **Orange earns its weight.** The brand orange (#F59B1B / oklch(0.72 0.18 45)) is distinctive and strong. Reserve it for what matters most: primary CTAs, key proof points, section anchors. When everything is orange, nothing is.

2. **Show transformation, not features.** The visitor's question is "will this change my business?" Lead with outcomes (ROI, efficiency gains, scale) rather than feature checklists. The design should make results feel real and near.

3. **Power without clutter.** This is a capable, complex product. The site should feel capable without being overwhelming. White space and editorial restraint signal confidence; busyness signals anxiety.

4. **Dark drives authority.** The deep navy background is a deliberate choice: it makes orange pop, creates contrast that generic light sites can't match, and projects the kind of seriousness that earns enterprise trust from an SMB audience.

5. **Spanish-first confidence.** Every word, label, and headline is written for a Mexican business professional. The tone is direct and outcome-focused, not warm-and-friendly SaaS. No filler words. No generic taglines.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast on body text, 3:1 on large text and UI components. Keyboard navigable. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations.
